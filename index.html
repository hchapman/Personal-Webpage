    About Me

    I am currently a mathematics graduate student at the University of Georgia working towards a Ph.D. degree. For the 2012-2013 school year, I am being supported by the University of Georgia's VIGRE II grant. I graduated from Bowdoin College in May of 2011 with a B.A. in Mathematics and Computer Science, with Honors in Mathematics.

    Research

    In the summer of 2010, I took part in an REU where we tried to answer questions about Sudoku (with M. Rupert, mentor E. Arnold): A Group-theoretic Approach to Human Solving Strategies in Sudoku. During my senior year at Bowdoin (2010-2011), I wrote an Honors thesis on orbital varieties in GL(n) and the Smith conjecture (advisor T. Pietraho): On orbital varieties of type A.

    My present research interests mostly lie within Topology.

    Interests

    Aside from math, I have a strong interest in computers. I'm always tinkering with my Linux machines and moving between small programming projects, ranging from websites to Android apps. I love open source (and git!) and have everything available on my github account. I've detailed my more substantial projects on the projects page.

    If you're the type of person to play favorites, this should help sum me up: My favorite editor is Emacs. My favorite mathematical software package is SAGE. My favorite programming language is Python (in fact, this webpage runs on it!). Feel free to argue with me if you disagree.

    Teaching

    For the 2012-2013 school year, I have no teaching duties. In the past, I have run recitations for calculus (Math 2200).
